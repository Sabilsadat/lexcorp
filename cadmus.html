PROJECT CADMUS

Founded as a stem cell research center, Project Cadmus (Previously Project 33.1) grew exponentially to become the worldwide leader in bio-engineering and mapping the human genome. Project Cadmus has made leaps in healthcare through the application of its genetic research, with thousands of patients worldwide being treated with state-of-the-art tissue regeneration technology.

MISSION STATEMENT
FROM OUR LEAD SCIENTIST

“Our goal has always been to save lives through testing the realm of genetic engineering.”

Dr. Emil Hamilton has been the lead researcher of Cadmus Labs since 2011. His work on Kryptonian Biology earned him several accolades, including the Luthor Foundation Prize and a Nobel Prize nomination.

CLONING AND GENOME RESEARCH

Project 33.1 was acquired by Lex Luthor in 2002. Under his leadership, it became the center for his early research into the effects of the meteor rocks (kryptonite) found in Smallville, Kansas.
However, once it got rebranded as Project Cadmus, it became primarily known for its achievements in genetic engineering and it's human cloning capabilities.
