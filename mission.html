HISTORY OF LEXCORP

LIST OF LEXCORP FACILITIES

Metropolis, Kansas

LexCorp Headquarters

LexCorp Tower is the international headquarters of LexCorp, located in downtown New Troy, Metropolis since its inception as LuthorCorp in the 1980s. It is the tallest building in the city, as well as one of the city’s most recognizable landmarks, a shining example of the feats and décor of the City of Tomorrow.

Smallville, Kansas

CADMUS LABS

Cadmus Labs is a scientific research facility that was once owned by LuthorCorp and now LexCorp located just outside of Smallville. This is the first iteration of Cadmus Labs.

Brooklyn, New York

T.C.R.I HEADQUARTERS

T.C.R.I. (Techno-Cosmic Research Institute) is a scientific corporation/organization with a large, mysterious and imposing facility in Brooklyn, New York City. It was an independent company from 1984 to 2003 prior to being absorbed by LexCorp.

Central City, Missouri

S.T.A.R LABS

Scientific and Technological Advanced Research Laboratories, or simply S.T.A.R. Labs, is a research and development company primarily located in University Town, Central City, founded by Harrison Wells. It became a division of LexCorp in 2014.

Merritt Island, Florida

KENNEDY SPACE CENTER,
LAUNCH COMPLEX 39A

LexCorp is honored to launch from Kennedy Space Center’s historic Launch Complex 39A, home of the Apollo and Space Shuttle programs. In addition to commercial satellite launches and space station resupply missions, LC-39A supports crew launches of the Excalibur spacecraft.

Coast City, California

LEX AIRWAYS

Development, manufacturing, testing, and launch of LexCorp’s Excalibur spacecraft and Super Heavy rocket takes place at Coast City, California. Lex Airways (previously Ferris Airways) was designed for orbital missions, and launches from Lex Airways will provide access to destinations in Earth orbit, the Moon, Mars, and beyond.
